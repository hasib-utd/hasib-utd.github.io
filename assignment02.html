Assignment 02: Prompt Exercise

Author

Muhammad Al Hasib

Objective

This assignment designs prompts to conduct a structured, systematic literature review on data mining and machine learning using multiple AI models (ChatGPT, Copilot, and Grok 3), analyzes each model’s contributions, and refines the prompts for academic precision and depth.

Step 1: Initial Prompt Creation

The following baseline prompt was submitted to ChatGPT, Copilot, and Grok 3:

“Conduct a 2,000-word structured,The systematic literature review on the applications of data mining and machine learning in real-world domains. Include a methodology section, synthesize key findings, identify trends and gaps, and propose one testable hypothesis. Use an academic tone and emulate systematic review standards.”

Step 2: Analyze Model Responses

ChatGPT Output

ChatGPT produced a broadly structured response covering multiple domains, including healthcare, finance, and education. It followed a systematic review format with clearly labeled sections: Introduction, Methodology, Key Findings, Trends and Gaps, and Hypothesis.

Strengths:

  • Comprehensive coverage across multiple domains
  • Well-organized structure with clear section headings
  • Proposed a testable hypothesis linking machine learning model complexity to prediction accuracy in healthcare diagnostics
  • Citations included authors and years (e.g., LeCun et al., 2015; Breiman, 2001)

Weaknesses:

  • Some citations could not be verified on Google Scholar
  • Synthesis of findings was broad but lacked depth in specific application areas
  • Trends section was somewhat generic without pinpointing recent developments post-2022

Copilot Output

Copilot delivered a more concise and tightly structured response. It excelled at organizing content into a clear, readable format and provided a well-defined methodology section referencing PRISMA guidelines.

Strengths:

  • Concise and well-structured output
  • Referenced the PRISMA systematic review methodology explicitly
  • Identified specific research gaps, such as a lack of interpretability in deep learning models
  • Citations were more verifiable and linked to well-known publications

Weaknesses:

  • Word count fell short of the 2,000-word target
  • Hypothesis was somewhat narrow, focusing only on natural language processing
  • Limited coverage of domains outside of healthcare

Grok 3 Output

Grok 3 offered a distinctive perspective with an emphasis on emerging trends and novel research directions. Its tone was academic but more exploratory compared to the other models.

Strengths:

  • Highlighted cutting-edge trends such as federated learning and explainable AI (XAI)
  • Proposed a bold and testable hypothesis about the role of transfer learning in low-resource settings
  • Provided fresh insights not commonly found in standard reviews

Weaknesses:

  • Structure was less formal compared to ChatGPT and Copilot
  • Methodology section was brief and lacked detail
  • Some references were difficult to verify

Step 3: Refined Prompts

Based on the weaknesses identified in each model’s response, the following tailored prompts were developed:

Refined Prompt for ChatGPT

“Write a 2,000-word structured, systematic literature review on the applications of data mining and machine learning in healthcare, finance, and education. Follow PRISMA guidelines for the methodology section. For each domain, synthesize at least three peer-reviewed findings published between 2018 and 2024. Identify two major research gaps and propose one specific, testable hypothesis. Ensure all citations follow APA format and are verifiable on Google Scholar.”

Improvement rationale: Adds domain specificity, a date range for sources, PRISMA requirement, and verifiable citation standards to address ChatGPT’s broad but shallow synthesis and unverifiable references.

Refined Prompt for Copilot

“Produce a 2,000-word systematic literature review on data mining and machine learning applications across at least four domains: healthcare, finance, education, and one additional domain of your choice. Use PRISMA methodology. Synthesize key findings in depth for each domain, identify at least three meaningful research gaps, and propose a testable hypothesis that spans multiple domains. Maintain a rigorous academic tone throughout.”

Improvement rationale: Expands domain coverage, enforces the word count, and broadens the hypothesis to address Copilot’s narrow focus and short output.

Refined Prompt for Grok 3

“Imagine you are a data scientist conducting a 2,000-word systematic literature review on how data mining and machine learning are applied in domains like healthcare, finance, and education. Outline a detailed PRISMA-based methodology section, synthesize key findings with fresh insights from post-2020 research, highlight emerging trends such as federated learning and explainable AI, identify critical research gaps, and propose one bold, testable hypothesis. Maintain a rigorous academic tone and include verifiable APA citations.”

Improvement rationale: Retains Grok 3’s strength in emerging trends while adding structure through PRISMA methodology and verifiable citations.

Comparison of Refined Outputs

After rerunning the refined prompts, all three models showed measurable improvement:

  • ChatGPT produced more domain-specific synthesis with verifiable citations
  • Copilot expanded its coverage and met the word count with a broader hypothesis
  • Grok 3 improved its methodology section while retaining its innovative perspective on emerging trends

Step 4: Cross-Model Collaboration

Synthesis Prompt

The following prompt was used to integrate the best elements from all three model outputs:

“Using the following drafts from three AI models [ChatGPT output, Copilot output, Grok 3 output], produce a 2,000-word structured systematic literature review on data mining and machine learning applications. Combine the strongest methodology from Copilot, the broad domain synthesis from ChatGPT, and the emerging trends insights from Grok 3 into a cohesive, academically sound document with verifiable APA citations and one bold, testable hypothesis.”

Final Synthesized Systematic Literature Review

Introduction

Data mining and machine learning (ML) have emerged as transformative forces across numerous real-world domains over the past two decades. From predictive diagnostics in healthcare to algorithmic trading in finance and personalized learning in education, the breadth of their applications continues to expand at an unprecedented pace. This systematic literature review synthesizes findings from peer-reviewed research published between 2018 and 2024 to examine the state of knowledge, identify trends and gaps, and propose a testable hypothesis for future inquiry.

Methodology

This review follows the Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) guidelines. A structured search was conducted across Google Scholar, Semantic Scholar, and PubMed using the keywords: data mining, machine learning, deep learning, healthcare, finance, education, and systematic review. Studies were included if they were peer-reviewed, published in English between 2018 and 2024, and directly addressed the application of data mining or ML in real-world domains. Studies lacking empirical results or methodological transparency were excluded.

Key Findings

Healthcare: Machine learning has demonstrated significant efficacy in clinical decision support, disease diagnosis, and patient outcome prediction. Deep learning models, particularly convolutional neural networks (CNNs), have achieved radiologist-level accuracy in medical imaging tasks such as tumor detection and diabetic retinopathy screening (Rajpurkar et al., 2022). Natural language processing (NLP) techniques have further enabled automated extraction of clinical insights from unstructured electronic health records.

Finance: In the financial domain, ML algorithms including gradient boosting, random forests, and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) have been widely applied to fraud detection, credit risk assessment, and algorithmic trading. Studies have shown that ensemble methods consistently outperform traditional statistical models in predicting loan defaults, with accuracy improvements of 10–15% (Lessmann et al., 2015; Bhatore et al., 2020).

Education: Educational data mining (EDM) has enabled institutions to predict student performance, identify at-risk learners, and personalize instructional content. Collaborative filtering and clustering algorithms have been used to build adaptive learning systems that respond dynamically to individual learning patterns (Baker & Inventado, 2014; Hussain et al., 2019).

Trends

Several notable trends have emerged across domains. First, federated learning has gained traction as a privacy-preserving approach that allows model training across decentralized data sources without sharing raw data — particularly relevant in healthcare (Li et al., 2020). Second, explainable AI (XAI) has become a critical area of focus as stakeholders demand interpretable predictions from black-box models (Arrieta et al., 2020). Third, transfer learning has significantly reduced the data requirements for training effective models in low-resource settings, enabling ML adoption in developing regions.

Research Gaps

Despite significant progress, several gaps remain. First, most studies are conducted in high-resource settings with large, well-labeled datasets, limiting generalizability to low-resource environments. Second, the lack of standardized benchmarks across domains makes cross-study comparisons difficult. Third, ethical concerns surrounding algorithmic bias, data privacy, and accountability remain underexplored in the literature.

Hypothesis

H1: The integration of explainable AI (XAI) frameworks into machine learning models deployed in high-stakes domains (healthcare, finance, and education) will significantly increase stakeholder trust and model adoption rates compared to equivalent black-box models, as measured by user acceptance surveys and deployment frequency in peer-reviewed case studies published between 2020 and 2025.

Conclusion

This review demonstrates that data mining and machine learning have achieved substantial real-world impact across healthcare, finance, and education. However, critical gaps in generalizability, interpretability, and ethical governance must be addressed to realize their full potential. Future research should prioritize explainable, privacy-preserving, and equitable ML frameworks.

Step 5: Reflection

How did each model approach the systematic review differently?

Each model brought a distinct approach to the systematic literature review task. ChatGPT prioritized breadth, providing comprehensive coverage across multiple domains but sacrificing depth and citation accuracy in the process. Its output read as a well-organized overview but lacked the specificity needed for rigorous academic work. Copilot, by contrast, excelled at structural precision — its familiarity with PRISMA guidelines and concise academic formatting made it the strongest model for producing a formally structured review, though it often fell short on word count and domain diversity. Grok 3 distinguished itself through its focus on emerging and cutting-edge trends such as federated learning and explainable AI, offering a more forward-looking perspective than the other two models. However, its methodology section was underdeveloped and its citations were harder to verify.

Which prompt refinements yielded the best results for each model?

For ChatGPT, adding a specific publication date range (2018–2024) and requiring APA-formatted, verifiable citations dramatically improved the academic quality of its output. For Copilot, specifying a minimum word count and requiring coverage of at least four domains resolved its tendency toward brevity and narrow focus. For Grok 3, the most effective refinement was explicitly requesting a PRISMA-based methodology section while encouraging it to retain its strength in emerging trends — this balanced structure with innovation effectively.

What did you learn about leveraging AI for structured academic reviews?

This exercise revealed that no single AI model is uniformly superior for all aspects of a systematic literature review. Each model has identifiable strengths and weaknesses that can be strategically leveraged through careful prompt engineering. The most valuable insight was that prompt specificity directly determines output quality — vague prompts produce generic responses, while detailed, criteria-driven prompts yield academically rigorous content. Cross-model collaboration, where the best elements of each output are synthesized, produced a final review that was stronger than any individual model could generate alone. This points to a promising workflow for AI-assisted academic research: use multiple models in parallel, evaluate outputs critically, and synthesize deliberately.
